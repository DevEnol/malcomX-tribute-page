A Tribute to one of the most important intellectuals of the 20th-century

Chronology of Malcom X life

  • May 19, 1925 Malcolm Little is born in University Hospital, Omaha, Nebraska.
  • 1929 The family’s home in Lansing, Michigan, is burned to the ground.
  • 1931 His father is found dead on the town’s trolley tracks.
  • 1946 Serving 8 to 10 years sentence at Charlestown State Prison for armed robbery.
  • 1948-49 Became a member of the Nation of Islam while in prison.
  • 1953 Changes his name to Malcolm X and becomes Assistant Minister of Nation of Islam’s Detroit Temple.
  • 1954 Becomes the Minister of Nation of Islam’s New York Temple.
  • 1958 Marries Betty X in Lansing, Michigan.
  • 1959 Travels to United Arab Republic, Sudan, and Nigeria, and Ghana.
  • 1963 Suspended from representing the Nation of Islam, allegedly because of remarks concerning President Kennedy’s assassination.
  • March, 1964 Malcolm X publicly announced his break from the Nation of Islam and starts his new organization, Muslim Mosque, Inc.
  • April, 1964 Malcolm X flew to Jeddah, Saudi Arabia, as the start of his Hajj, the pilgrimage to Mecca and visited Africa after.
  • May, 1964 Forms the Organization of Afro-American Unity (OAAU), a secular group that advocated Pan-Africanism.
  • February 14, 1965 Malcolm X’s home is firebombed in the early morning.
  • February 21, 1965 Malcolm X is assassinated right after he begins speaking at the Audubon Ballroom, New York.
